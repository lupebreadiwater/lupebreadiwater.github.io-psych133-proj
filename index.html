Effects on Body Effects on Brain

Is pulling all-nighters bad? What happens when I’m sleep deprived? Is sleep important?

Let’s take a journey and find out more about these questions. This website is designed to teach you about the consequences of sleep deprivation and the benefits of getting proper sleep.

Getting a short amount of sleep may help you finish your essay today, but it does come at a cost. Beware of the consequences related to sleep deprivation because even one day can have a lasting impact...

Sleep deprivation effects on the body:

  1. Sleep deprivation renders vaccination shots useless 💉. Even after recovery sleep, individuals were never to produce its lost antibodies (Prather, Pressman, Miller, & Cohen, 2020 ↗).
  2. Less sleep also increases your chances of getting sick 🤧 after being exposed to a virus (Prather, Janicki-Deverts, Hall, & Cohen, 2015 ↗).
  3. Short sleep (less than 7-9 hrs) has been labeled as a “probable” carcinogen, according to World Health Organization in 2007 💊. It may increase the risk of developing cancer. In the case where cancer is already present, it can cause cells to accelerate and grow more quickly (Gozal, 2015 ↗).
  4. Sleep loss alters the hormones that regulate glucose metabolism 🍴. Many studies show an increase in “ghrelin” which causes individuals to crave and eat carb-heavy foods. As a result, there’s a higher chance of developing type II diabetes and obesity from insufficient sleep (Duraccio, Krietisch, Chardon, Van Dyk, & Beebe, 2019 ↗).
  5. Turns out “beauty sleep” is, in fact, true 💤. A study here found that participants were rated much more attractive when they had enough sleep compared to when they were sleep deprived (Sundelin, Lekander, Sorjonen, & Axelsson, 2017 ↗). Although subjective, there does seem to be a change in your physical appearance that others may notice.

Sleep deprivation effects on the brain:

  1. Prolonged sleep deprivation might not directly kill you but its effects can certainly be fatal 🚗.
    • A study done on rats found that sleep loss led to the rat’s death (Everson, 1989 ↗). However, the exact cause was immune failure after ~20-45 days depending on REM or NREM sleep loss, respectively.
    • Car accidents caused by lack of sleep can be more deadly than by drugs or alcohol. Don’t get wrong, both are extremely dangerous, but the difference comes with reaction time. When you're driving intoxicated or high, you respond slower, but when you’re sleepy you have NO response in an accident (Arnedt, Owens, Crouch, Stahl, & Carskadon, 2005 ↗).
    • If you’re in the medical field, please listen up, these are some jaw-dropping statistics for working 30hr shifts (Walker, 2018 ↗):
      • 460% increase in more diagnostic errors in the intensive care unit
      • 170% increase risk of major surgical errors
      • 1/5 medical residents cause serious harm to a patient due to fatigue
      • 1/20 medical residents will KILL a patient due to fatigue
      • 25,655 residents in training = 1,000+ deaths/year

  2. You need sleep before + after learning to encode and consolidate new memories and information 🛏 (Rasch & Born, 2013 ↗).
  3. Sleep helps to consolidate and prevents forgetting of fact-based memories 📝 (Rasch & Born, 2013 ↗), so reconsider the next time you want to pull an all-nighter and instead make sure you get enough sleep before your exams.
  4. Attention becomes impaired over time with sleep deprivation 💭 (Alhola & Polo-Kantola, 2007 ↗).
  5. Sleep helps stabilize your emotional mental state + mood ‼️ , so not getting proper sleep can lead to an amplified emotional response and mood disturbances (Dinges, 1997 ↗).
  6. Mood disorders report having chronic sleep problems 😴 (Necklemann, 2007 ↗).
  7. Irregular sleep is also linked to psychiatric disorders such as depression, anxiety, PTSD, schizophrenia, and suicide (Krystal, 2012 ↗).
  8. Sleep deprivation can make you more irritable, aggressive, emotionally unstable, and paranoid 😡 (Peter Tripp case study ↗).
